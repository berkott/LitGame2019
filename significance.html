Significance and Theme

Elsinore's Palace of Games was developed to show the theme of ones own mortality. This is illustrated in all of the games since eventually, the player dies in all of them. There is an end to all of the games just like there is to any life on earth. This is seen throughout the entire play as Hamlet questions his mortality and the purpose of his existence. He most directly addresses this concern during his infamous To be or not to be soloiquy where he says, "To die, to sleep-To sleep, perchance to dream. Ay there's the rub," (3.1.72-73). Here Hamlet expressed his fear of the unknown in death when he says, "Ay there's the rub," and also expresses his thoughts about killing himself when he says "To die, to sleep." Overall, the theme of mortality is seen throughout the whole novel where Hamlet constantly questions his mortality through thoughts of suicide. Other characters such as Hamlet's ghost father and Claudius also show the significance of mortality in the play by killing and seaking power.
